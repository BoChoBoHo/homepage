Current zoning laws in Boulder forbid more than 3 (or 4) unrelated people from living together, regardless of how many bedrooms are in the home.

This means that a 5 bedroom house can never be legally fully occupied: there will always be empty bedrooms (unless a family lives there).

We consider this enforced wastefulness, because bedrooms are for people.

We’re working to amend the law so that each bedroom in Boulder can legally be occupied.

Why?

There is a housing crisis in Boulder: prices are through the roof, both for renting and for buying. Bedrooms are forced to remain empty, wasting heat and power.

Our ballot initiative is a simple way to increase the supply of bedrooms in Boulder (allowing people to share costs and live more efficiently) without incurring the costs—environmental and financial—associated with construction.

Current phase

We’re currently collecting signatures to put this initiative on the ballot in November, where City of Boulder residents will have a chance to vote on it.
